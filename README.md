## Simone Wien, PhD, MPH
I am an epidemiologist working as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the [University of California, Irvine, Wen School of Population & Public Health](https://publichealth.uci.edu/).

My current work focuses on how changes in policy (e.g., laws that criminalize prenatal and postpartum substance use) and health services (e.g., advances in neonatal intensive care) affect outcomes across the reproductive life course, including pregnancy loss, birth outcomes, and maternal morbidity and mortality. Methodologically, I characterize challenges in observational study design for reproductive and perinatal research and develop strategies to address them.

I received my PhD and MPH in Epidemiology from the Rollins School of Public Health, Emory University, where I was a [NIDA T32 Predoctoral Fellow](https://scholarblogs.emory.edu/rollinssudprogram/tada-program/tada-program-requirements/) and an [ARCS Foundation Scholar](https://arcsfoundation.org/).
